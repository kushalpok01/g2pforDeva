Etymology

[edit]

Sometimes explained as a Sanskritization of Prakrit 𑀡𑁂𑀯𑀸𑀮 (ṇevāla), compare Newar नेवाः (newā:, “Newar”). Others regard the Sanskrit form as older. Perhaps ultimately of Sino-Tibetan origin. More on Wikipedia.

Popular etymologies derive it from compounds like ने (ne, “a chief”) +‎ पाल (pāla, “protector”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • (Classical Sanskrit) IPA(key): /n̪eː.pɑː.l̪ɐ/

Proper noun

[edit]

नेपाल • (nepāla) stem, m

  1. Nepal (an ancient kingdom in South Asia, roughly coextensive with modern Nepal)
  2. Nepal (a country in South Asia, located between China and India)
  3. the Nepalese
